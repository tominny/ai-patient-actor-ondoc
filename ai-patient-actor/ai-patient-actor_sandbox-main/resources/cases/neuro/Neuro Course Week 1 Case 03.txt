Diagnosis: Carpal tunnel syndrome
1. **Patient Demographics**
   - **Name**: Sarah Johnson
   - **Age**: 52 years
   - **Sex**: Female
   - **Occupation**: Seamstress

2. **Presenting Complaint**
   - Pain, numbness, and tingling in the right hand and wrist, especially in the thumb, index, and middle fingers.

3. **History of Present Illness**
   - Sarah has been experiencing symptoms for about six months. She reports that the symptoms are worse at night, often waking her from sleep, and in the morning. She occasionally feels the need to "shake out" her hand to relieve the symptoms. The symptoms seem to be exacerbated by extensive sewing.

4. **Past Medical History**
   - Hypertension, managed with medication.

5. **Medications and Allergies**
   - **Medications**: Lisinopril 10 mg daily
   - **Allergies**: No known allergies

6. **Family History**
   - No significant family history of neurological or rheumatological diseases.

7. **Social History**
   - Sarah is a seamstress and spends several hours a day working with fabrics and sewing. She is right-handed. She does not smoke and consumes alcohol socially.

8. **Review of Systems**
   - No recent illness, fever, or unintentional weight loss. No other joint pains or muscle weaknesses.

9. **Physical Examination**
   - **Vitals**: BP 130/85 mmHg, HR 75 bpm, RR 16/min, Temp 98.7°F
   - **General**: Sarah appears comfortable, not in acute distress.

10. **Neurological Examination**
   - **Motor Examination**:
      - Normal muscle bulk in both upper extremities.
      - No atrophy or fasciculations noted.
      - Strength 5/5 in all muscle groups except for the right abductor pollicis brevis, which is 4/5.
   - **Sensory Examination**:
      - Decreased sensation to light touch in the median nerve distribution of the right hand.
   - **Reflexes**:
      - Brachioradialis, biceps, and triceps reflexes 2+ bilaterally.
   - **Special Tests**:
      - Tinel’s sign: Positive over the right carpal tunnel.
      - Phalen’s test: Produces tingling in the median nerve distribution of the right hand.

11. **Diagnostic Tests**
   - **X-ray right wrist**: No signs of arthritis or fracture.
   - **Electromyography (EMG) and Nerve Conduction Studies (NCS)**:
      - Median nerve sensory and motor latencies are prolonged at the wrist, consistent with carpal tunnel syndrome.
